About Expert

Expert is a responsive web app helping users search for an expert in a variety of fields and book a video call with them immediately (within 15 minutes of booking) or at a future time. When the expert creates their Expert Information page, in addition to posting a bio, they can attach videos and articles that further allows users to determine if the expert’s knowledge meets their needs.

This project was created for my Career Foundry UX Immersion certificate.

Goal

To create a seamless and efficient user experience for individuals seeking immediate expert assistance in areas such as academics, DIY, and music.

Design Approach

Competitor Analysis

A competitor analysis was conducted on PrestoExperts and Chegg, Both sites had thousands of registered experts, but Chegg primarily focused on academia while PrestoExperts brands itself as a mental health and educational assistance site.

Marketing Profile

PrestoExperts promotes itself through social media networks such as Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter as well as coming up on the first page of an internet search, PrestoExperts also bring users to its site through the use of an affiliate program.

Marketing Advantage

Presto Experts has clients and experts from all over the world. Sessions are billed by the minute (experts set their own billing rates) so most people are able to get assistance.

Usability

Finding an expert on PrestoExperts is not difficult, but overwhelming. If the user is general in their search, they will be provided with a large number of experts to look through. If the profile is not well-written, the user will have to click into each profile to see if each expert’s background meets the user’s needs.

PrestoExperts Home Page

Marketing Profile

In addition to having a social media presence through Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter, Chegg also markets itself through an affiliate program. Chegg also has many other products, services, and network affiliates that it cross-markets such as eTextbooks, Chegg Writing, Busuu, and Internships.com.

Marketing Advantage

Chegg primarily markets its services to students, educators, and career advancers/changers. Because of Chegg’s and its other entities focusing on academic enrichment, they are a good one-stop-shop for academics seeking assistance.

Usability

The site is extremely well laid-out and the use of pictures/graphics is helpful to understand Chegg’s offerings.

User Research

Initially, I interviewed 5 people in-person or over Skype to learn how they seek help with confronted with an academic or DIY problem. I spoke with men and women whose ages ranged from 19-54 and spoke with both students and DIYers as that was the initial target audience of Expert.

Research Goals

  • Identify users’ reasons for seeking an expert.
  • At what point would they seek one?
  • When and why would they want the contact to be immediate?
  • Determine what factors bring users to contacting an expert.
  • What features would bring users to the Expert app?

Sample Questions

For DIYers

  • What kind of projects have you done in the past?
  • How successful was it?
  • Did you seek help before starting?

For Students

  • Have you ever needed additional resources other than what was provided by your professor?
  • Do you ever seek out a person for additional help or stick with written materials and online videos?

Highlights from Interviews

  • Over half of the people who do DIY projects do not consider themselves to be DIYers.
  • DIYers are generally not planners; they work on the project and get additional tools and materials as they go along.
  • Students know early on in their studies if they need additional materials or help.
  • Students are more likely to do due diligence on their sources than DIYers.
  • Students tend to verify information through multiple sources.
  • None of the interviewees stated they used more than one source, if they used any.

Wireframing, Prototyping, and Additional Testing

After creating the personas, the next step was to start making wireframes and prototypes. After those were complete, another round of user testing took place along with preference testing. The feedback was invaluable and helped to create a better designed process that was more intuitive to use.

Of the many functions in Expert app, booking a call and payments were greatly enhanced through the iterative design and testing process.

Book a Video Call

The ”book a video call” frame has changed in many ways to present a streamlined booking experience to the user.

  • If the user wants to book a call now, the button has a CTA. Also, the user is informed of a waiting period of up to 15 minutes for the expert to join the call.
  • The calendar was also enhanced to be cleaner.

Design Reflections

From the time I sat down to sketch out my initial prototype for the Expert app until now, my design has improved in leaps and bounds. In addition to being more robust, I have learned so much about layout in terms of color theory, proper use of font size, and designing for accessibility. This information, combined with the user testing I conducted, really helped me come up with better drafts and I feel that each iteration brings the Expert app towards something that would be fun to bring to market.
